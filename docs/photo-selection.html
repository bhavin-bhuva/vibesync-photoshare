Photo Selection

Photo selection replaces the old email-and-spreadsheet workflow. Clients browse your gallery, tap the photos they love, add optional notes, and submit the list. You receive a notification, review the picks, download the chosen files, and deliver. No filenames, no confusion.

What is Photo Selection?

Photo selection is a feature of the public gallery that lets clients identify which photos they want you to deliver. Rather than scrolling through a gallery and trying to remember filenames or screenshot thumbnails, clients tap directly on the photos they want — the gallery marks them with a checkmark. When done, they submit the list with their name and any notes.

As the photographer, you receive a notification and can see exactly which photos each client chose, review them in context, download the selected files as a ZIP, and track the delivery status from your dashboard.

Enabling Selection Mode

Selection mode is available on all share links by default — clients can always enter it by clicking Select Photos in the gallery. There is no per-link toggle to disable it.

ℹ️
Selection persists across sessions

A client's in-progress selections are saved to their browser's localStorage against the gallery slug. If they close the tab and return later, their previously tapped photos are still marked. The selection is only cleared after they submit.

How Clients Select Photos

  1. Enter the gallery

    Client opens the share link and enters the PIN or password. They land on the gallery view showing all photos.

  2. Click "Select Photos"

    On desktop, this toggle is in the mode bar at the top of the gallery. On mobile, a floating button appears in the bottom-left corner. Tapping either switches the gallery into selection mode.

  3. Tap photos to select them

    Each tap toggles the photo's selection state. A filled blue checkmark appears on selected photos; tapping again removes it. The count in the sticky bottom bar updates with every tap.

  4. Add a note per photo (optional)

    On a selected photo, clients can long-press (mobile) or right-click (desktop) to open a note sheet. They can leave a short message — e.g. "Love this one but prefer without the background person" — that you'll see alongside the photo in your selection review.

  5. Click "Add details" in the bottom bar

    Expand the submit bar to reveal the name, email, and overall note fields.

  6. Enter name and submit

    Name is required; email and note are optional. Clicking Submit Selection sends the list and shows a confirmation screen. The gallery then shows the submitted selection in read-only mode.

Viewing Client Selections

When a client submits a selection, PhotoHouse sends an email notification to you (if email is configured) and places a red badge on the Selections button in your event header. Click the button to open the Selections page.

Submission cards

Each card on the Selections page represents one submission and shows:

  • Client name and email — as they entered in the submit form.
  • Submission timestamp — date and time the form was submitted.
  • Overall note — the client's free-text message, if any.
  • Per-photo notes — individual notes attached to specific photos, shown as a tooltip on each thumbnail.
  • Selected photo thumbnails — a grid of the photos the client picked.
  • Status badge — see below.

Status workflow

Each submission moves through three statuses:

  • PENDING — new submission, not yet reviewed.
  • REVIEWED — you've opened and acknowledged the submission.
  • DELIVERED — selected photos have been sent to the client.

Click the status badge on a submission card to cycle it to the next state.

Downloading Selected Photos

From the Selections page, click Download All Selections to generate a ZIP containing every photo chosen across all submissions for that event. Pro Studio

ZIP organisation by group

If the event has photo groups, the ZIP is organised into sub-folders matching the group names. Photos not assigned to any group are placed in an Ungrouped folder. This means you can open the ZIP and immediately navigate to the section you need to retouch and deliver.

ℹ️
ZIP download requires Pro or Studio

Free accounts can view and review selections but cannot download the ZIP. Individual selected photos can be downloaded one at a time from the photo lightbox on any plan.

Watermark on download

If watermarking is enabled in your Studio Profile, selected photos downloaded via the ZIP are watermarked — identical behaviour to individual photo downloads from the gallery. See Watermarking for configuration details.

Marking as Delivered

Once you've edited and sent the final files to the client, update the submission status to DELIVERED. This helps you keep track of which jobs are complete across multiple events.

  1. Open the Selections page for the event

    Click the Selections button in the event header.

  2. Find the submission card

    Locate the client's submission by name or date.

  3. Click the status badge and set to Delivered

    The badge updates immediately. The change is saved to the database — refreshing the page retains the status.

Client notification

PhotoHouse does not currently send an automatic email to clients when you mark a selection as Delivered. To let the client know their photos are ready, send a separate message with your delivery link — whether that's a download link, WeTransfer, Google Drive, or another delivery method you prefer.

If your platform has email configured via Amazon SES, the new selection submitted notification email is sent automatically to you when a client submits. Outbound client notifications are on the product roadmap.
