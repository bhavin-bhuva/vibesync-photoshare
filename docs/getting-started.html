Getting Started

PhotoHouse is a client gallery platform for professional photographers. Upload your photos, organise them into groups, and share a branded gallery link with your clients — all in a few clicks. This guide gets you from a fresh account to your first delivered gallery.

Introduction

PhotoHouse is built around a simple idea: a photographer should be able to share a beautiful, private gallery with a client in under five minutes. Everything in the platform — upload queues, photo groups, PIN-protected share links, face search, watermarking — exists to support that core workflow.

Who is it for? PhotoHouse suits any photographer who delivers digital images to clients: wedding photographers, portrait and headshot studios, corporate event photographers, and real-estate shooters.

  • Events map to individual shoots. One wedding = one event.
  • Photo groups let you divide an event into sections (Ceremony, Reception) that clients can filter.
  • Share links give each client a private, PIN- or password-protected URL.
  • Photo selection lets clients pick their favourites and submit the list to you.
  • Face Search (Pro/Studio) lets clients find every photo of themselves using a selfie.

Creating Your Account

All new accounts start on the Free plan — no credit card required. You can upgrade to Pro or Studio at any time from the Billing page.

  1. Open the registration page

    Navigate to /register in your browser. You'll see the PhotoHouse sign-up form.

  2. Fill in your details

    Enter your full name, email address, and a password (minimum 8 characters). Your name appears on your studio profile and in notification emails sent to clients.

  3. Verify your email

    Check your inbox for a verification message and click the link inside. Unverified accounts cannot create share links.

  4. Log in

    Return to /login, enter your credentials, and you'll land on the main dashboard.

💡
Use a professional email

Notification emails to your clients are sent from the address configured by the platform. Your account email is only used for login and system alerts.

Setting Up Your Studio Profile

Before sharing galleries, add your studio branding so every gallery your clients see reflects your identity — not a generic PhotoHouse default.

Go to Dashboard → Profile → Studio Branding.

What to configure

  • Studio name — shown in gallery hero headers and outgoing notification emails to clients.
  • Logo — upload a square PNG or JPG (400×400 px recommended). Appears in gallery heroes, PIN/password entry screens, and — if watermarking is on — as the watermark graphic on downloaded photos.
  • Tagline — a one-line description shown beneath your studio name on gallery pages (e.g. "Capturing moments that last forever").
  • Brand colour — used as the gallery hero background when no cover photo is set. Enter any hex value.
  • Website, phone, address — optional contact details shown in gallery footers.

Watermark settings

On the same Profile page, scroll to Watermark Settings. You can enable the watermark toggle, set the position (bottom-right, bottom-left, or bottom-center), and adjust opacity from 10% to 80%. A live preview updates as you drag the slider. See Watermarking for full details.

ℹ️
Watermarking requires Pro or Studio

The watermark toggle is available on Pro and Studio plans. Free accounts serve downloads without any watermark.

Understanding Your Dashboard

The dashboard is your home screen. It shows a high-level summary of your studio and lets you navigate to any event.

Stats bar

Four cards at the top of the dashboard give you a quick overview:

  • Events — count of events created vs. your plan limit.
  • Photos — total photos across all events.
  • Shared links — number of active (non-revoked, non-expired) gallery links.
  • Storage — a progress bar showing bytes used vs. your plan's limit.

New selections banner

When a client submits a photo selection, a blue banner appears at the top of the dashboard with a direct link to review it. The banner disappears once you open the Selections page for that event.

Event grid

Each card in the grid represents an event. The card shows the cover photo (or a colour gradient placeholder), event name, date, group count, and how many active share links the event has. Click any card to open the event.

Navigation

The left sidebar (or mobile menu) gives you access to:

  • Dashboard — the home screen you're on now.
  • Billing — your plan, storage, and invoice history.
  • Profile — personal info, studio branding, watermark settings.

Your First Event

An event groups all the photos from one shoot. Create one for every job you deliver.

  1. Click "+ New Event" on the dashboard

    The New Event modal slides in from the right.

  2. Enter a title

    Use a name that identifies the shoot clearly — e.g. "Smith Family Portrait — Dec 2024". Clients see this name in their gallery header.

  3. Set the shoot date

    The date is shown in the gallery header and used to sort events on your dashboard.

  4. Click Create

    You're taken directly to the new event page, ready to upload photos.

💡
Include the year in the name

When you have hundreds of events over several years, titles like "Johnson Wedding 2024" are easier to search than "Johnson Wedding".
