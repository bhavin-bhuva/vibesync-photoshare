Billing & Plans

PhotoHouse has three plans — Free, Pro, and Studio. All billing is handled by Stripe; your card details are never stored on the PhotoHouse server. You can upgrade, downgrade, or cancel at any time from the Billing page in your dashboard.

Upgrading Your Plan

You can upgrade at any time — there's no need to wait for a billing cycle to end. Stripe handles the proration automatically; you'll only be charged the difference for the remaining days in your current period.

  1. Navigate to the Pricing page

    Go to /pricing to see all available plans with current prices. Prices are managed by the platform admin and may vary from the default.

  2. Click "Get started" on your chosen plan

    You'll be redirected to Stripe Checkout — a secure, PCI-compliant payment page hosted by Stripe.

  3. Enter your payment details

    Stripe accepts all major credit and debit cards. Your card number is never sent to or stored by PhotoHouse.

  4. Complete the payment

    Stripe sends a webhook to PhotoHouse confirming the subscription. Your account is upgraded within seconds — no manual approval required.

💡
Upgrade from the Billing page too

If you're already logged in, go to Dashboard → Billing and click Upgrade plan to reach the same Stripe Checkout flow without navigating to the public Pricing page.

Managing Your Subscription

Go to Dashboard → Billing and click Manage billing. This opens the Stripe Customer Portal — a secure, Stripe-hosted page where you can manage every aspect of your subscription without leaving your browser.

Stripe Customer Portal lets you

  • Update your payment method — add a new card, remove an old one, or set a different card as the default.
  • Switch billing interval — move between monthly and annual billing. Annual billing typically includes a discount.
  • Change or cancel your plan — downgrade to a lower tier or cancel entirely.
  • Download invoices — every invoice is available as a PDF from the portal's invoice history.

Cancellation

When you cancel, your subscription remains active until the end of the current billing period. After that, your account reverts to the Free plan automatically — no data is deleted.

⚠️
Storage over the Free limit after downgrade

If your stored photos exceed the Free plan's 5 GB limit, new uploads are blocked. Existing photos are not deleted — you'll need to either delete some events to bring storage under the limit, or re-upgrade to continue uploading.

Storage Usage

How storage is calculated

Storage usage is the sum of the original file sizes of every photo across all your events. The calculation uses the size of the original uploaded file, not the thumbnail.

What counts toward storage

  • Original photo files (JPEG, PNG, WebP, TIFF)
  • Cover photo files uploaded for each event
  • Studio logo file (minimal — typically a few KB)

What does not count toward storage

  • Generated thumbnails (stored in S3 but not counted in your quota)
  • Face embeddings and face crop thumbnails
  • Client selfies uploaded for face search (deleted after 24 hours)

When storage is full

When you reach your plan's storage limit, the upload modal shows a Storage limit reached error and new uploads are blocked. Existing photos, events, and share links remain fully functional. To resume uploading, either:

  • Delete events or photos to free up space, or
  • Upgrade to a plan with a higher storage limit.

A warning banner appears on your dashboard when storage exceeds 90% of your limit, giving you time to act before uploads are blocked.

Invoices & Receipts

Past invoices are displayed directly on the Dashboard → Billing page, fetched in real time from Stripe. Each row in the invoice table shows:

  • Date — the invoice creation date.
  • Description — the plan name and billing interval (e.g. Pro Plan — Monthly).
  • Amount — total charged, including any applicable taxes.
  • Status — paid, open, or void.
  • PDF — a download link for the official Stripe invoice PDF, suitable for expense reports and VAT returns.
ℹ️
Invoices are also available in the Stripe Customer Portal

Click Manage billing on the Billing page to open the Stripe portal, where you can access the full invoice history including any invoices predating your PhotoHouse Billing page integration.
