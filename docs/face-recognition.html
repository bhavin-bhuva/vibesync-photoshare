AI Face Search

Face Search lets clients upload a selfie and instantly see every photo in the gallery that contains their face — no scrolling through hundreds of images. For photographers, it reduces the time clients spend hunting for themselves in large wedding or event galleries.

Enabling Face Detection

Face detection must be enabled per event. Open an event, click the People tab, then click Enable Face Detection.

PhotoHouse will queue an indexing job that:

  1. Scans every photo in the event for faces
  2. Extracts a 512-dimension face embedding for each detected face
  3. Clusters similar faces into people clusters

Indexing time depends on event size. A 200-photo wedding typically completes in under 2 minutes. A progress bar shows the status.

People Clusters

After indexing, the People tab shows a grid of face clusters — one cluster per person detected in the event. Click a cluster to see all photos containing that person.

Labelling people

Click the pencil icon on a cluster to give it a name — e.g. "Bride", "Groom", "Uncle Raj". Labels are visible only to you (photographers), not to gallery clients.

Hiding a cluster

Toggle the visibility of a cluster to prevent its photos from appearing in client face search results while keeping them in the main gallery.

Find My Photos — Client Experience

To enable Find My Photos on a gallery share link, open the Share modal, toggle Allow Face Search, and save. Face detection must be complete before you can enable this toggle.

Clients with a face-search-enabled link see a Find My Photos button in the gallery. Tapping it opens a modal where they upload a selfie. PhotoHouse compares the selfie embedding against indexed faces and returns matching photos within seconds.

💡
Privacy note

Face embeddings are numerical vectors stored in your database — no facial image is sent to a third-party service. All processing runs on your own infrastructure.

Deleting Face Data

At the bottom of the People tab, click Delete All Face Data. This removes all embeddings, clusters, and face records for the event. The underlying photos are not affected. Face detection can be re-enabled afterwards.
