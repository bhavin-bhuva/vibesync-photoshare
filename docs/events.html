Managing Events

Events are the core unit in PhotoHouse — each event maps to a single shoot and holds all photos, groups, and client share links. Learn how to create, organise, and manage your events.

Creating an Event

From the dashboard, click the + New Event button. Give the event a descriptive name and set the shoot date — the date appears on your gallery header and helps you sort events.

ℹ️
Event limits

Free accounts are limited to 3 events. Pro accounts get 25. Studio accounts have unlimited events. See Billing & Plans.

Organising with Photo Groups

Groups let you divide an event's photos into named sections — Ceremony, Reception, Portraits — so clients can browse and filter by section in the gallery.

Creating a group

Open an event, then look for the group filter bar above the photo grid. Use the group manager to add a new group, assign it a colour, and drag photos into it.

Group filter pills

Once groups exist, filter pills appear above the photo grid. Clicking a pill shows only photos in that group. Clients see the same pills in their gallery.

Hiding groups

Toggle a group's visibility to hide it from clients without deleting it. The group still appears in your photographer view with a strikethrough label.

Setting a Cover Photo

The cover photo appears as the gallery hero and as the thumbnail on your dashboard event card. To set one, click the cover photo zone at the top of any event page and upload or select a photo.

💡
Best dimensions

Landscape photos at 1920×1080 px or wider look best. The hero crops to a 16:9 ratio.

Grid Density

Use the density control (top-right of the photo grid) to switch between four layouts:

  • Comfortable — 2 columns, large previews. Great for reviewing detail.
  • Default — 3 columns. Balanced view for most events.
  • Compact — 4 columns. Good for large events.
  • Dense — 6 columns. Maximum overview for culling hundreds of photos.

Photo Lightbox

Click any photo to open the lightbox. Navigate with ← → arrow keys, or swipe on mobile. Press Esc to close.

The info panel (ⓘ button) shows EXIF metadata — camera model, focal length, aperture, shutter speed, and ISO — when available.

Deleting an Event

Open the event's ⋯ More menu and choose Delete Event. Deletion is permanent — all photos are removed from S3 and all share links are revoked.

⚠️
This cannot be undone

Deleting an event removes all its photos, groups, share links, and client selections permanently. Download any photos you want to keep first.
